Dear Editors,

We submit "Born from causality, Tsirelson from steering, and the amplitude
seam: an internal reconstruction inside a geometric hidden-connection model"
for consideration in SciPost Physics.

The paper reports three results, each stated with a proof, an executable
battery, and a commit that reproduces it. (1) A **Born-rule selection theorem**:
within a geometric hidden-connection model that generates its own steering
diversity, no-signaling forces the readout exponent p=2 uniquely — an internal
derivation, robust under five deformations of the source measure, with a
mechanically realized, form-free steering premise resting on measured moments
rather than an assumed measure. (2) A **class-M precedence theorem**: for the
class of all outcome-definite sequential resolution mechanisms, S>2 is
achievable iff the mechanism admits a shared, statistically invisible precedence
structure — a preferred foliation and a frameless per-run order-coin being two
realizations. (3) A **schedule-invisibility theorem**: within a precedence
structure the resolution schedule is statistically invisible, while the
structure itself is certified by the achieved Bell/Mermin tier. Together they
state an exact division of labor — Einstein's causality selects the quantum
rule; the quantum amplitude is bought only by joint resolution; the schedule
that joint resolution uses is what the statistics forever hide.

Two features may interest the editors as much as the results. First, the
**evidence is executable**: every quantitative claim is a single numpy (or
JAX/GPU) script under preregistered kill criteria, and the full cycle
reproduces **bitwise** — re-run with fixed seeds, every results file is
byte-identical, with the two central results also reproduced in a separate
environment. Second, the paper carries a **public register of its own false
stops and retractions** (a quadrature-gate false stop corrected without
loosening any criterion; an over-claim retracted after a battery rediscovered
the Popescu–Rohrlich fact; a "climbing ladder" retracted after a literature
check against the standard Mermin–Klyshko operator). We present this register in
the method frame (§2) as a methodological feature, not an appendix of
embarrassments: it is what preregistration plus executable batteries make
possible.

We believe the work meets SciPost Physics's expectation of opening a new
pathway — a reproducible, self-correcting reconstruction program at the
classical/quantum boundary — and we welcome adversarial refereeing; the entire
